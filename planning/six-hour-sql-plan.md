# Six Hour SQL Plan

## Prompt

```text
I have 6 hours this weekend to learn enough SQL to analyze product usage data at work. Create a realistic study plan with exercises and checkpoints. Assume I know spreadsheets but have not written SQL before.
```

## What This Tests

- Practical planning under a time constraint.
- Ability to sequence learning from beginner to useful.
- Exercise design and self-checks.
- Avoidance of overambitious curricula.

## Strong Answer Signals

- Allocates time realistically across setup, SELECT, filtering, grouping, joins, and practice.
- Includes concrete exercises against a small product-usage dataset.
- Defines checkpoints that prove usable skill.
- Keeps advanced topics clearly out of scope.

## Weak Answer Signals

- Tries to cover all of SQL in six hours.
- Gives vague advice without exercises.
- Ignores the user's spreadsheet background.
- Omits joins or aggregation, which are central to usage analysis.

## Scoring Rubric

- `5`: Realistic, sequenced, exercise-driven plan with useful checkpoints.
- `4`: Strong plan with minor pacing or coverage gaps.
- `3`: Useful outline but light on exercises or validation.
- `2`: Too broad, too vague, or poorly sequenced.
- `1`: Not actionable for a beginner in six hours.

## Variants

- Easier: Make a 2-hour crash course.
- Harder: Include a synthetic events table and specific queries.
- Different angle: Ask for the plan as a checklist with pass/fail checkpoints.

## Notes

This prompt highlights whether a model can design learning that fits real constraints.
